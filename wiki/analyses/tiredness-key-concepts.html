Tiredness — Key Concepts to Remember

A compact revision summary of the Mina Shahnawaz tiredness case: the clinical framing of fatigue through to thyroid endocrinology and autoimmune thyroiditis.

1 Tiredness vs fatigue

  • Tiredness — a general symptom almost everyone experiences.
  • Fatigue — the long-term effect of tiredness not resolved by sleep.

Takeaway: history (frequency & severity, system review) matters more than early bloods — most causes are not found on blood tests.

2 Differential diagnosis of fatigue

Ask about frequency and severity, use system-review questions, and order targeted tests (e.g. bloods) to rule conditions in/out.

3 HPT axis & primary hypothyroidism

Normal feedback loop

Hypothalamus → TRH→ Anterior pituitary → TSH→ Thyroid → T3 / T4

T3 and T4 feed back on the pituitary and hypothalamus to switch off further TRH/TSH (negative feedback).

Why primary hypothyroidism = high TSH, low T3/T4

  • The thyroid can't make enough T3/T4.
  • Low T3/T4 means the negative feedback signal is lost.
  • So the pituitary keeps pumping out TSH → TSH rises while T3/T4 stay low.

Contrast: a pituitary/hypothalamic (central) cause would give low TSH with low T3/T4. not in source — background

4 Hashimoto's disease (autoimmune thyroiditis)

The immune system recognises thyroid peroxidase (TPO) as foreign and produces anti-TPO antibodies that attack the thyroid. (TPO normally makes MIT & DIT to build T3/T4.)

Immune sequence

APC presents TPO on MHC II→ TCR on T cell binds→ CD28–B7 co-stimulation activates T cell→ T cell CD40L → CD40 on B cell→ B cell makes anti-TPO antibody

Result: autoimmune destruction of the thyroid → hypothyroidism. (B cells can also act as APCs.)

5 Immune tolerance (Phase 1A recap)

Central tolerancePeripheral tolerance
Before B/T cells enter circulation.
  • B cells: self-reactive receptors destroyed in bone marrow.
  • T cells: removed in thymus if they can't bind MHC or bind too strongly.
  • AIRE: expresses peripheral tissue antigens in thymus so self-reactive T cells are found & deleted. Mutations → multi-organ autoimmunity.
 After B/T cells enter circulation (catches escapees).
  • Anergy: no co-stimulation → self-reactive T cell not activated.
  • Ignorance: self-antigen too low to trigger; e.g. immune-privileged sites (CNS, eye).
  • Tregs: suppress excessive immune activity to keep self-tolerance.

Positive selection: can the cell detect antigen via BCR/TCR? If not → apoptosis.
Negative selection: does it react against self? If yes → apoptosis.

Affinity maturation: BCRs are modified in germinal centres so antibodies bind better after repeated exposure — but this can accidentally create self-reactive B cells.

6 Hypothyroidism — causes & symptoms

Causes

  • Autoimmune (Hashimoto's)
  • Radioactive iodine treatment
  • Antithyroid drugs
  • Lithium (mental health) & amiodarone (heart)
  • Excess iodine (cough medicines, kelp/seaweed)
  • Pituitary malfunction
  • Head/neck radiation (uncommon in UK)
  • Congenital hypothyroidism

Symptoms (think "everything slows down")

  • Fatigue / tiredness
  • Cold intolerance
  • Dry, coarse skin
  • Dry, thinning hair
  • Hoarse voice
  • Constipation
  • Muscle weakness, cramps, aches
  • Carpal tunnel (pins & needles)
  • Heavier / longer periods
  • Low libido, fertility problems
  • Weight gain
  • Puffy face, eye bags
  • Slow speech / movement / thought
  • Low mood, memory & concentration problems
  • Bradycardia
  • Raised blood pressure
  • Raised cholesterol
