# Thyroid Peroxidase (TPO)

## What it is
Thyroid peroxidase (TPO) is the thyroid enzyme used to produce MIT and DIT in the synthesis of thyroid hormones T3 and T4, and the target autoantigen in Hashimoto's disease (see [Mina Shahnawaz – Tiredness](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

## Properties
- **Type:** molecule (enzyme / autoantigen)
- **Owner / Operator:** thyroid gland
- **Status in our context:** the self-antigen attacked in autoimmune thyroiditis.

## Description
- TPO produces **MIT and DIT**, precursors in the production of T3/T4 (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- It is normally present in the thyroid gland of all healthy individuals (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- In [Hashimoto's disease](./hashimotos-disease.md), TPO is recognised as foreign, and anti-TPO antibodies are produced against it (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

## Relationships
- Produces: precursors (MIT/DIT) for T3/T4 — see [HPT axis](../concepts/hpt-axis.md)
- Targeted by: [Hashimoto's disease](./hashimotos-disease.md)

## Open questions
