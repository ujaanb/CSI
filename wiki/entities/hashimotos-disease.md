# Hashimoto's Disease

## What it is
Hashimoto's disease is an autoimmune condition in which the immune system recognises thyroid peroxidase (TPO) as foreign and mounts an anti-TPO antibody response that attacks the thyroid, a cause of autoimmune hypothyroidism (see [Mina Shahnawaz – Tiredness](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

## Description
### Immune sequence
- Physiologically, the **TPO antigen** ([thyroid peroxidase](./thyroid-peroxidase.md)) is present in the thyroid gland (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- In Hashimoto's, TPO is recognised as foreign and an immune response is elicited against it (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- An **antigen-presenting cell (APC)** presents MHC II with the TPO antigen, interacting with the **TCR** on T cells (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- A **co-stimulatory interaction** between the T cell's **CD28** and the **B7** ligand on the APC helps activate the T cell (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- The activated T cell presents **CD40L**, which stimulates B cells by interacting with the **CD40 receptor** on B cells (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- B cells recognise CD40L and are induced to produce the **anti-TPO antibody**, which attacks the thyroid (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- B cells can also act as APCs (an alternative pathway) (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

### Consequence
Ongoing autoimmune attack impairs thyroid hormone output, producing [hypothyroidism](./hypothyroidism.md).
