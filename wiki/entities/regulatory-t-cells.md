# Regulatory T Cells (Tregs)

## What it is
Regulatory T cells (Tregs) are a T cell subset essential for self-tolerance and immune homeostasis, preventing autoimmune disease by suppressing excessive immune activity (see [Mina Shahnawaz – Tiredness](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

## Properties
- **Type:** cell
- **Owner / Operator:** immune system
- **Status in our context:** a peripheral-tolerance mechanism reviewed as Phase 1A background.

## Description
- Tregs maintain immune homeostasis and tolerance, preventing autoimmune diseases and regulating immune responses (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- Their ability to **suppress excessive immune activity** is critical for self-tolerance (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- They also play significant roles in cancer, transplantation, and infectious diseases (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

## Relationships
- Part of: [immune tolerance](../concepts/immune-tolerance.md) (peripheral tolerance)
- Failure implicated in: autoimmune conditions such as [Hashimoto's disease](./hashimotos-disease.md)
