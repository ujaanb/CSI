# Immune Tolerance

## What it is
The set of mechanisms that prevent the immune system from attacking the body's own cells, split into central tolerance (before lymphocytes enter circulation) and peripheral tolerance (after they enter circulation) (see [Mina Shahnawaz – Tiredness](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)). Reviewed here as Phase 1A background relevant to autoimmune thyroid disease.

## Description
### Self vs non-self recognition
- PAMPs help cells distinguish pathogens from self cells (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- Central and peripheral tolerance together ensure self-reactive cells are removed or restrained (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

### Central tolerance
- Acts on B and T cells **before** they are secreted into circulation (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- **B cell selection:** differentiating B cells patrol the bone marrow for self antigens; a B cell whose receptor recognises a self antigen is destroyed before release (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- **T cell selection:** T cells that cannot bind MHC, or that bind MHC too strongly, are removed in the thymus (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- **AIRE (Autoimmune Regulator):** transcription factors in the thymus that express peripheral tissue antigen genes so self-reactive T cells can be recognised and destroyed (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)). AIRE mutations prevent adequate T cell selection and cause multi-organ autoimmunity (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- Central tolerance is simpler for B cells than T cells: B cells bind free antigens (location in the bone marrow can be used to select them), whereas T cells only bind antigens presented on MHC, making selection more complex (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

### Peripheral tolerance
- Acts **after** B/T cells enter circulation, catching self-reactive cells that slipped through central tolerance (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- **Anergy:** host-cell MHC lacks the co-stimulatory molecules needed to activate naive T cells, so self-reactive T cells are not activated and become less likely to activate in future (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- **Ignorance:** the self-antigen concentration is too low to trigger destruction, so self-reactive T cells are effectively ignored; relevant in immunologically privileged areas (e.g. CNS, eye, brain) where autoimmunity risk outweighs infection risk (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- **Regulatory T cells (Tregs):** suppress excessive immune activity to maintain self-tolerance and immune homeostasis (see [Regulatory T cells](../entities/regulatory-t-cells.md)).

### Positive and negative selection
- **Positive selection:** tests whether a B/T cell can detect antigens via its BCR/TCR; cells that cannot are developmentally abnormal and undergo apoptosis (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- **Negative selection:** tests whether a B/T cell reacts against self antigens; self-reactive cells undergo apoptosis (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
