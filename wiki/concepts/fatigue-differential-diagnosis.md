# Differential Diagnosis of Fatigue

## What it is
The structured approach to identifying the cause of fatigue/tiredness, combining a symptom history with targeted system-review questions and investigations to rule conditions in or out (see [Mina Shahnawaz – Tiredness](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

## Description
### Approach
- Ask about the **frequency and severity** of symptoms; this is often very helpful for gauging impact (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- Use other system-related questions and tests (e.g. bloods) to establish what conditions to rule out (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- Note that history often outperforms early blood testing: <10% of fatigue presentations have a condition detected on blood tests (see [Tiredness vs Fatigue](./tiredness-vs-fatigue.md)).

### Candidate causes
Possible causes of fatigue named in the case (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)):
- Anaemia (including iron deficiency)
- Sleep apnoea
- Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid)
- Coeliac disease
- Chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS)
- Diabetes
- Glandular fever
- Depression
- Restless legs syndrome
- Anxiety
- Malignancy
- Vitamin D deficiency
- Heart failure
- Infection
- Lupus

### Non-pathological contributors
Insufficient/poor sleep, unhealthy lifestyle, stress and life challenges, hormonal changes (puberty, pregnancy, menopause), and medical treatments (e.g. chemotherapy) (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
