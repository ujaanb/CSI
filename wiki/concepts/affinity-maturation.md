# Affinity Maturation

## What it is
The process by which B cell receptors are progressively modified so the antibodies they produce bind a pathogen more tightly after repeated exposure (see [Mina Shahnawaz – Tiredness](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

## Description
- The BCR is constantly modified to produce slightly different antibodies, and the lymph nodes select those that best fit the antigen (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- It occurs in **germinal centres** (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- **Consequence:** because the antibodies are being modified, some B cells can end up recognising self antigens and attacking self cells (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)) — a link between normal antibody optimisation and autoimmunity.

## Relationships
- Related to: [Immune tolerance](./immune-tolerance.md)
- Can contribute to: autoimmune responses such as [Hashimoto's disease](../entities/hashimotos-disease.md)

## Open questions
- The source states affinity maturation can lead to self-recognition but does not detail how peripheral tolerance normally contains this risk.
