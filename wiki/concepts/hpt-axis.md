# Hypothalamic–Pituitary–Thyroid (HPT) Axis

## What it is
The negative-feedback hormonal loop that regulates thyroid hormone output, running from the hypothalamus to the anterior pituitary to the thyroid gland (see [Mina Shahnawaz – Tiredness](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

## Description
### Normal physiology
- The hypothalamus releases **TRH** (thyrotropin-releasing hormone), which causes the anterior pituitary to release **TSH** (thyroid-stimulating hormone) (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- TSH causes the thyroid to release **T3 and T4** (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- T3 and T4 act back on the anterior pituitary and hypothalamus to suppress further TRH and TSH secretion — a negative feedback loop (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).

### Primary hypothyroidism (high TSH, low T3/T4)
- In primary hypothyroidism, TSH is still released but the thyroid cannot release adequate T3/T4 (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- With low T3/T4, the negative feedback signal is lost, so the hypothalamus/pituitary are not told to stop — TRH and TSH keep being secreted (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)).
- Result: **high TSH** in the bloodstream alongside **low T3/T4** (see [source](../sources/mina-shahnawaz-tiredness.md)). This biochemical pattern is the hallmark distinguishing primary (thyroid-level) from central (pituitary/hypothalamic) hypothyroidism.

## Relationships
- Regulates: thyroid hormone output
- Explains: the biochemistry of [Hypothyroidism](../entities/hypothyroidism.md)
- Disrupted by: [Hashimoto's disease](../entities/hashimotos-disease.md)

## Open questions
- The case covers primary hypothyroidism only; the contrasting pattern in secondary/central hypothyroidism (low TSH, low T3/T4) is inferred background and not stated in the source (inferred — no direct source).
